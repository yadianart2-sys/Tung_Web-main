Meme Coin Tokenomics Explained — Supply, Burns, Reflections & Liquidity

If you're new to meme coins, you've probably seen terms like "total supply," "liquidity lock," "auto-burn," and "reflection." Understanding meme coin tokenomics is essential before investing in any crypto project. In this guide, we break down every component using TUNG as a real-world example on BNB Chain.

2. Auto-Burn Mechanism (Deflationary)

A deflationary token permanently removes coins from circulation, increasing scarcity over time. TUNG implements an auto-burn on every transaction — 1.5% of every buy and sell is sent to a dead wallet. Starting with 2,000,000 tokens already burned, the circulating supply constantly decreases.

Why Auto-Burn Matters

As tokens are burned, each remaining token represents a larger percentage of the total supply. If you hold 100,000 TUNG today and 10% of supply gets burned over time, your ownership percentage increases by 10% — plus you still earn reflections on every transaction.

3. Reflection Dividends (Passive Income)

Reflection is the mechanism that makes passive income crypto possible. TUNG charges a 6% fee on every transaction:

  • 3% redistributed to all holders as dividends
  • 1.5% auto-burn
  • 1.5% marketing & ecosystem

These dividends accumulate in your wallet automatically with zero effort.

4. Liquidity Lock

Liquidity is the lifeblood of any token. TUNG has 53% of liquidity locked, meaning the team cannot withdraw the trading pool. This prevents rug pulls and provides stability for traders. Locked liquidity is verified through Pinksale and visible on BSCScan.

5. Staking Rewards

Beyond reflections, TUNG allocates 3,000,000 tokens (9.52%) for staking rewards on Falco-X Swap. Stake your TUNG tokens to earn additional yield on top of your automatic dividends — creating a powerful double-earning strategy.

Why Tokenomics Matter for Meme Coins

The best meme coins of 2026 — like SHIB, PEPE, and TUNG — all have one thing in common: well-designed tokenomics. A strong tokenomics model ensures:

  • Sustainable price floor through burns and locked liquidity
  • Incentives to hold long-term through reflections
  • Reduced selling pressure through staking mechanisms
  • Community trust through transparent allocation
